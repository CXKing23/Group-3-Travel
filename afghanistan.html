Afghanistan

Our Story

Afghanistan, a land of rugged beauty and deep history, has been a crossroads of civilizations for centuries. From the towering mountains of the Hindu Kush to the ancient ruins of Buddhist and Persian empires, Afghanistan offers a glimpse into a fascinating and resilient culture.

At Travel Adventure, we aim to highlight Afghanistan’s cultural heritage, from the historic city of Herat to the breathtaking Band-e Amir lakes. Despite its challenges, Afghanistan remains a place of incredible landscapes and warm hospitality.

Language

The official languages of Afghanistan are Dari (Persian) and Pashto. Many Afghans also speak Uzbek, Turkmen, and other regional languages.

Must-see Attractions

1. Kabul – The capital city, rich in history, bustling bazaars, and cultural sites like the National Museum.

2. Bamiyan – Home to the remnants of the ancient Buddhas of Bamiyan and stunning mountain landscapes.

3. Herat – A historic city known for its stunning mosques, including the Great Mosque of Herat.

4. Band-e Amir – Afghanistan’s first national park, featuring a series of breathtaking blue lakes.

5. Mazar-i-Sharif – Famous for the Blue Mosque (Shrine of Ali), one of the most beautiful Islamic sites in the world.

Visa Facts

PASSPORT VALIDITY: Must be valid for at least six months from the date of entry.

BLANK PASSPORT PAGES: At least one blank page required.

TOURIST VISA REQUIRED: Yes, all visitors need a visa.

VACCINATIONS: Recommended vaccinations include hepatitis A, typhoid, and polio.

CURRENCY RESTRICTIONS FOR ENTRY: Must declare amounts over $10,000 USD.

CURRENCY RESTRICTIONS FOR EXIT: Must declare amounts over $10,000 USD.
